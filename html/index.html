Trainer checklist

Small details that make a stronger team

Cover weaknesses

A good team has answers for its own weak spots. If two favorites share a weakness, add a teammate that resists or threatens that type.

Mix roles

Try combining fast attackers, bulky defenders, status users, and support Pokemon instead of choosing only high-damage favorites.

Watch abilities

Abilities can change a battle before a move is used. Weather, immunity, speed, healing, and contact effects can all swing momentum.

Plan movesets

Four moves should work together. Include same-type attacks, coverage moves, status options, or setup moves depending on the Pokemon's role.

Think about items

Held items can boost power, restore HP, protect against status, or help a Pokemon survive a hit it normally could not handle.

Build personality

The strongest team is not always only about numbers. Themes, favorite colors, matching Pokeballs, and region identity make teams memorable.
