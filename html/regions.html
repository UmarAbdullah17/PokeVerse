World guide

Pokemon Regions

Learn the major identity, story focus, culture, and adventure style of each main Pokemon region.

Kanto Johto Hoenn Sinnoh Unova Kalos Alola Galar Paldea
Original League

Kanto

Kanto is the classic starting point of the Pokemon world. It has a simple but powerful structure: small hometowns, forests, caves, coastal cities, eight gyms, and a final Pokemon League challenge. Its tone is adventurous and direct, making it feel like the foundation that later regions build on.

Important themes include friendship with starter Pokemon, Team Rocket's criminal activity, gym badge progression, and the discovery of legendary Pokemon hidden in places like caves, power plants, and islands.

History and Tradition

Johto

Johto feels older and more spiritual than Kanto. Its towns are closely tied to legends, towers, ceremonies, and traditional architecture. The region is famous for Ho-Oh, Lugia, the Burned Tower, the Bell Tower, Apricorn Pokeballs, and a slower, more historic atmosphere.

Johto also connects directly back to Kanto, which makes it feel like a sequel region. Its story suggests that Pokemon history is not just about battle power, but also about memory, respect, and the relationship between people and legendary Pokemon.

Nature and Weather

Hoenn

Hoenn is shaped by land, sea, and sky. It has tropical routes, underwater areas, volcanic zones, forests, deserts, and many coastal towns. Its legendary story focuses on Groudon, Kyogre, and Rayquaza, making the region strongly connected to natural balance.

The region also adds contests and secret bases, giving players more ways to interact with Pokemon beyond battling. Hoenn's biggest idea is that nature is powerful, beautiful, and dangerous when balance is broken.

Myth and Creation

Sinnoh

Sinnoh is mountainous, cold, ancient, and deeply mythological. It explores the origin of the Pokemon universe through legends connected to Arceus, Dialga, Palkia, and Giratina. Its ruins, lakes, temples, and old stories make it one of the most lore-heavy regions.

Sinnoh's main conflict is not just about winning battles; it is about what happens when someone tries to control reality itself. That makes the region important for understanding time, space, antimatter, and creation myths in Pokemon.

Truth and Ideals

Unova

Unova is a faraway region with a more modern identity. It includes large cities, bridges, industrial areas, deserts, forests, and a story that asks whether trainers and Pokemon truly understand each other.

The region's main themes are truth, ideals, freedom, and responsibility. Through Team Plasma and the legendary dragons, Unova asks one of the biggest questions in Pokemon: are people helping Pokemon, using them, or both?

Beauty and Power

Kalos

Kalos is built around beauty, fashion, art, cafes, grand cities, and dramatic history. It introduced Mega Evolution, which adds a special bond-based power between trainers and Pokemon.

The darker side of Kalos is its ancient weapon and the idea that beauty and power can become dangerous when people try to control life itself. This gives Kalos a stylish surface with surprisingly heavy lore underneath.

Islands and Ultra Space

Alola

Alola is a tropical island region that replaces traditional gyms with island trials. Its culture is shaped by nature, local customs, guardian deities, and close relationships between people and Pokemon.

Alola is also one of the strangest regions because of Ultra Wormholes and Ultra Beasts. It combines relaxed island life with cosmic mystery, making it both peaceful and deeply weird in the best way.

Stadium Culture

Galar

Galar treats Pokemon battles like huge public sports events. Gyms are stadiums, leaders are celebrities, and trainers compete in front of massive crowds. Its towns and cities mix countryside charm with industrial history.

The region's lore focuses on Dynamax energy, ancient heroes, royal legends, and the danger of exploiting power without fully understanding it. Galar is about spectacle, tradition, and energy beneath the surface.

Academy and Exploration

Paldea

Paldea is centered around an academy and open exploration. Instead of following only one path, trainers study, travel, battle Titan Pokemon, investigate Team Star, challenge gyms, and eventually uncover the mysteries of Area Zero.

Its biggest lore ideas are Paradox Pokemon, Terastal energy, and the blurred line between ancient past, distant future, and scientific imagination. Paldea feels like a region about curiosity and discovery.

Trainer routes

Pick your region by adventure style

Classic badge journey

Choose Kanto, Johto, or Unova if you want a clear gym path, strong rival energy, and a traditional league climb.

Nature expedition

Choose Hoenn, Alola, or Paldea if you like oceans, islands, open routes, unusual habitats, and Pokemon shaped by environment.

Lore-heavy mystery

Choose Sinnoh, Kalos, Galar, or Paldea if you want ancient myths, hidden power sources, legends, and big history questions.
